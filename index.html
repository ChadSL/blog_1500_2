China claims youth gaming addiction resolved

Posted on: November 24, 2022.


BBC News | November 23, 2022

According to the China Game Industry Group Committe, young people in China have gotten over their addictions to video games. Since August in 2021 China has banned all children from gaming for more than three hours a week. This was done as part of China's crackdown on their technology sector. Affected by this was one of the world's biggest gaming companies, Tencent. According to reports 75% of children in China now play for less than 3 hours a week which they consider to be "remarkable results".

San Francisco to allow police 'killer robots'

Posted on: November 30, 2022.


BBC News | November 30, 2022

San Franciso's police force have recently been approved to use lethal robots. The new measure also allows police to deploy robots equipped with explosives in "extreme circumstances". SFPD have said that they currently do not operate robots at lethal force, but they already have approval to do so. Some think this is good as this will allow police to engage dangerous situations without risk to their own lives. Those opposed to the new measure fear this is a slippery slope to further militarisation of the police force. The measure does however limit the robots to high-ranking officers and are only allowed to be used after empolying alternative de-escalation tactics.
